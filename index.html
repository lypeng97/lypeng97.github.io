Hi! I am a Ph.D. student in Biostatistics at the School of Public Health, University of Pittsburgh. I am advised by Dr. Tingting Zhang. Prior to attending the Ph.D. program, I completed my M.S. in Biostatistics at Emory University, under the guidance of Dr. Benjamin Risk. I obtained my B.S. in Statistics at Wuhan University.

My research focuses on developing novel statistical and machine learning methods for high-dimensional data, with a particular emphasis on applications in neuroimaging. My current work aims to provide new statistical tools for quantifying sources of variability and to offer fresh insights into the relationship between brain structure and function.

As a graduate student researcher at the CTSI, University of Pittsburgh, I collaborate with clinical researchers to analyze complex datasets using advanced statistical and machine learning methods for causal inference, meta-analysis, and personalized decision-making. I also work with the Silva lab on large-scale neuroimaging projects investigating life-span changes in the marmoset brain.

I am actively looking for full-time data scientist and research scientist positions. Let me know if you feel I am a good fit.
